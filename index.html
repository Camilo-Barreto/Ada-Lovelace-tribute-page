Ada Lovelace

Mathematician and writer

Key Events:

  • 1815: On 10th December, Augusta Ada Byron (Ada Lovelace) is born in London, England.
  • 1816: Five weeks after Ada was born, Ada's mother separates from Lord Byron and takes Ada to her parents' home.
  • 1824: Ada's father, Lord Byron, dies in Greece when she is eight years old.
  • 1835: On 8th July, Ada marries William King. They have three children: Byron (born 1836), Anne Isabella (born 1837), and Ralph Gordon (born 1839).
  • 1833: Ada meets Charles Babbage through her mentor, Mary Somerville. She is introduced to Babbage's work on the Difference Engine.
  • 1838: William King becomes the Earl of Lovelace, and Ada becomes the Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1842-1843: Ada translates an article by the Italian mathematician Luigi Menabrea about Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine. She adds extensive notes to the translation, which include what is considered the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine.
